Hi, I'm Seoyul Jeon, currently pursuing my B.S. in Computer Science (with a minor in Electrical Engineering) at KAIST. I am currently a Research Intern at the Sensing Intelligence and Cyber-Physical Security (CyPhy) Lab.

My primary research interests lie in the intersection of wireless communication, cyber-physical systems, and artificial intelligence. Recently, I have been working on developing a **Wi-Fi CSI-based Presence Detection System** utilizing the IEEE 802.11bf standard. 

I am passionate about building robust systems from the ground up and exploring advanced ML paradigms. My previous experiences include:
* Developing a **Deep Autoencoder-based Network Anomaly Detection System** entirely in C++ without external ML libraries.
* Analyzing the **AlphaZero AI Engine (MCTS)** and studying **LLM Alignment (DPO)** at the FlexML Lab.
* Designing a user identification system via **IMU sensors** embedded in remote controls, which was presented as a poster at MobiSys.

I enjoy solving complex algorithmic challenges and optimizing deep learning models for real-world applications.
